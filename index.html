Organic Farming Practices

Transitioning to organic practices improves crop nutrition, preserves biodiversity, and builds soil structure.

🪱

Composting & Vermicomposting

Convert farm waste into nutrient-rich black gold. Composting increases soil organic matter, improves water retention, and populates the soil with beneficial microbes.

  • Green Waste: Nitrogen rich (leaves, stalks, peels)
  • Brown Waste: Carbon rich (straw, woodchips, paper)
  • Vermicomposting: Utilizes earthworms to speed decomposition
🔄

Smart Crop Rotation

Prevent pests and crop disease cycle by shifting planting families. Alternating nitrogen-demanding crops with nitrogen-fixing plants naturally builds soil fertility.

  • Nitrogen Fixers: Legumes, beans, peas
  • Heavy Feeders: Maize, wheat, brassicas
  • Root Crops: Carrots, potatoes, beets
🐞

Bio-Pesticides & Bio-Fertilizers

Protect crops using nature's defenses instead of synthetic poisons. Implement biological barriers, trap crops, and natural formulations.

  • Neem Formulations: Repels over 200 chewing pests
  • Trichoderma: Fungi protecting roots from diseases
  • Rhizobium: Soil bacteria fixing atmospheric nitrogen
